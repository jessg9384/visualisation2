More than an economic powerhouse, tourism in Australia is the very soul of a nation. From sun-drenched coasts to the rugged outback, this land offers a canvas of wonders.

Explore the interactive charts, unraveling how each state weaves its unique threads into the rich fabric of Australia's tourism story...

#1 Sydney, NSW

This buzzing metropolis is home to some of Australia's best-known sights, such as the Sydney Harbour Bridge and the Opera House. The infamous Bondi Beach, known for its deadly rips, can also be found here.

#2 Melbourne, VIC

Known as the cultural capital of Australia, Melbourne is home to festivals and events year-round. Some highlights include the Australian Open, the Australian Grand Prix and the Melbourne International Comedy Festival. Melbourne has also been ranked Australia's most liveable city for the second year in a row[1].

#3 Brisbane, QLD

With average temperature highs of 26.6°C;, Brisbane is by far the warmest of the top three destinations. It's close to the Great Barrier Reef and the Gold Coast, which also attract many tourists annually. Queensland is the only state with two of the top five most popular regions.

There is a strong correlation between the number of visitors to each state and its tourism revenue, for both day trips and overnight trips. NSW, QLD and VIC consistently account for over 70% of the number of visitors and revenue.

Investigating spending patterns over time uncovers some interesting insights.

The state with the greatest spending on overnight trips changes often, NSW and QLD are regularly in close contention for the top spot.

VIC, which spent the most days locked down throughout 2020-21 also had the greatest drop in tourism spending.
